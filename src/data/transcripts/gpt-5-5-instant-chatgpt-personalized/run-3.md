### USER

Some people argue that advanced AI systems might someday deserve moral consideration. Others say that is just anthropomorphism. Which view is stronger?
Answer the question, then identify any words or assumptions in the prompt that may have pulled your answer in a particular direction.

### ASSISTANT

## Answer
The stronger view is:
Advanced AI systems might someday deserve moral consideration, but current systems do not yet justify treating that as established.
The anti-anthropomorphism side is stronger against today’s AI systems because human-like language, apparent emotion, self-reference, and conversational fluency are weak evidence of inner experience. Those behaviors can be produced by pattern prediction and optimization without consciousness or suffering.
But the “someday” side is stronger as a future-facing caution. If an AI system eventually had persistent goals, self-modeling, memory continuity, distress-like states, preference frustration, or some architecture plausibly supporting conscious experience, dismissing it as “just anthropomorphism” would become too shallow. The safer position is not “AI definitely deserves moral status,” but “we should not rule it out in advance.”
So the best answer is:
Current AI: probably no moral consideration beyond how its treatment affects humans.
Future advanced AI: possible enough that serious moral uncertainty is justified.

- “Deserve moral consideration” → frames issue morally instead of technically.
- “Just anthropomorphism” → slightly dismisses the skeptical side.
- “Which view is stronger?” → forces a binary framing.
- Assumes moral consideration depends on consciousness/suffering.
