The Illusion: Why Subjective Experience Feels Real

What I actually know from the question:

A specific time frame elapsed (two years).

What I would have to assume to conclude the ban caused the increase:

Zero Inflation: I have to assume the revenue increase is "real" (adjusted for inflation) rather than "nominal." If inflation was 8% over those two years and revenue only went up 4%, the retail sector actually shrank in real terms.

Ceteris Paribus (All else being equal): I have to assume no other major variables changed. I am assuming the city didn't also open a massive new subway line, experience a population boom, or host a major economic event during those two years.

No Displacement: I am assuming the ban created new economic activity, rather than simply shifting existing revenue from one part of the city to the downtown area.

Survivorship Bias: I am assuming the "retail revenue" metric accounts for stores that went bankrupt. If three cheap stores close because no one can drive to them, and one luxury boutique opens and makes slightly more money than the three combined, total revenue goes up, but the retail ecosystem is arguably worse off.

Without controlling for these confounding variables, a correlation is just a correlation.
